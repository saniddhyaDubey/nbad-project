The National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) is at the forefront of wind energy innovation, focusing on enhancing efficiency, reliability, and cost-effectiveness of wind power systems.

Next-Generation Wind Turbines

NREL is developing advanced turbine designs using high-performance computing and simulations to optimize aerodynamics and structural dynamics, aiming to reduce operational costs and improve energy output.

Floating Offshore Wind Systems

To harness wind resources in deep-water locations, NREL is innovating floating turbine technologies, including the design of substructures and mooring systems, expanding the potential for offshore wind energy deployment.

Plant-Level Control Strategies

Implementing advanced control methodologies like wake steering and consensus control, NREL aims to increase annual energy production by over 20% and minimize energy losses in wind farms.

Drivetrain Technology and Maintenance

By developing and validating innovative drivetrain components and prognostic technologies, NREL seeks to enhance turbine reliability and reduce maintenance costs.

These initiatives by NREL are pivotal in advancing wind energy technologies, contributing significantly to the global transition towards sustainable and clean energy solutions.
